📋 ERM Workflow — Stages

StageWhat Happens
1. IdentificationRisks are identified, documented with risk statement, contributing factors, and ownership assigned
2. AnalysisInherent likelihood and impact are analyzed across five capitals
3. AssessmentInherent Risk Score (IRS) is calculated using the formula and mapped to a rating
4. Response / TreatmentControls are assigned, action plans are created with defined ownership and target dates
5. Residual Risk AssessmentResidual Risk Score (RRS) is calculated — after factoring in the effectiveness of controls and treatment actions
6. ApprovalRisk and treatment plans are reviewed and approved by Risk Owner / Risk Champion
7. Monitoring & ReassessmentOngoing tracking; residual risk is periodically reassessed
8. Archival ApprovalBefore archiving, the Risk Owner / Risk Champion reviews and approves the archival request. An Archival Rationale must be documented to justify why the risk is being archived.
9. ArchiveOnce approved, risks are archived. Retained for audit trail and can be reactivated if conditions change.
Risk Score Formula
( Impact × Likelihood ) + Velocity
Risk Rating Scale
High ≥ 18   Medium 8–17   Low / Acceptable < 8

StrategyDescription
TreatImplement controls to reduce the likelihood or impact of the risk
TolerateAccept the risk within appetite — monitor but take no further action
TransferShift the risk to a third party (e.g., insurance, outsourcing)

📊 Phase 2 — Inherent (Gross) Risk Assessment

Score the risk BEFORE any controls are applied to determine its inherent severity.

Financial Capital

10a-1
Total Revenue Impact Dropdown
Score the impact on Total Revenue using a 5-point dropdown (Very Low, Low, Medium, High, Very High). The detailed percentage bands from the Financial Capital Risk Impact Scale (e.g., reduction of targeted revenue by ≤ 1.5%, > 1.5%–3%, > 3%–5%, > 5%–7.5%, > 7.5%) will be provided for reference as a tooltip on the Financial Capital Impact field.
10a-2
EBITDA Impact Dropdown
Score the impact on EBITDA using the same 5-point dropdown (Very Low, Low, Medium, High, Very High). The underlying ranges (e.g., reduction of targeted EBITDA by ≤ 4%, > 4%–8%, > 8%–12%, > 12%–16%, > 16%) will be captured as descriptive text in the tooltip attached to Financial Capital Impact, not repeated here.
10a-3
Leverage (Net Debt / EBITDA) Impact Dropdown
Score the impact on Leverage (Net debt to EBITDA, medium term) using the 5-point dropdown (Very Low, Low, Medium, High, Very High). The reference thresholds (e.g., ≤ 1x, > 1–2x, > 2–3x, > 3–4x, > 4x) will also be provided for reference as a tooltip against the Financial Capital Impact field.
10a
Financial Capital Impact Dropdown
Overall inherent impact on Financial Capital on a 5-point scale (Very Low, Low, Medium, High, Very High). A tooltip on this field will consolidate all reference descriptions from the Financial Capital Risk Impact Scale (Total Revenue, EBITDA and Leverage parameter bands) so that the assessor can view the detailed criteria while selecting the impact level.
10a-4
Risk Appetite / Tolerance Dropdown
Auto-populated classification based on Financial Capital impact and thresholds: Within risk appetite, Risk appetite breached, but within risk tolerance, or Risk tolerance breached. This field will be calculated along with the Final Impact Score after considering the values recorded against each of the capital impact fields (10a–10e).

Multi-Capital Inherent Assessment

For all capitals (Financial, Compliance, Human, Natural, Intellectual), inherent impact will be scored through parameter-based 5-point dropdowns (Very Low to Very High), with underlying criteria documented as reference tooltips. Capital-specific risk appetite and tolerance thresholds will be configured so that appetite / threshold calculations can be performed consistently across each capital and fed into the overall Final Impact Score.

10b
Compliance Capital Impact Dropdown
Impact on regulatory & legal compliance — Very Low (1) to Very High (5), using parameter-based scoring with capital-specific appetite and tolerance thresholds similar to Financial Capital.
10c
Human Capital Impact Dropdown
Impact on people, safety, and workforce — Very Low (1) to Very High (5), with parameter-based scoring and defined appetite / tolerance thresholds for Human Capital.
10d
Natural Capital Impact Dropdown
Impact on environmental and natural resources — Very Low (1) to Very High (5), supported by parameter-based scoring and Natural Capital appetite / tolerance thresholds.
10e
Intellectual Capital Impact Dropdown
Impact on intellectual property, R&D, and knowledge assets — Very Low (1) to Very High (5), with parameter-based scoring and appetite / tolerance thresholds defined for Intellectual Capital.
10
Final Impact Score Auto-Calculated
Highest among 10a–10e. The maximum impact score across all five capitals is used as the final Impact. This also drives the automatic population of the Risk Appetite / Tolerance outcome based on the appetite and tolerance thresholds configured for each capital.
11
Inherent Likelihood (1–5) Dropdown
Probability of occurrence — Unlikely 1%–20% (1), Rare 21%–40% (2), Possible 41%–60% (3), Likely 61%–80% (4), Almost Certain 81%–99% (5)
12
Velocity (1–5) Dropdown
Speed of impact — Very slow / over a year (1), Several months to a year (2), Few months to a quarter (3), Days to few weeks (4), Very rapid — few hours to days (5)
13
Inherent Risk Score Auto-Calculated
= (Impact × Likelihood) + Velocity — auto-calculated

📉 Phase 4 — Residual Risk Assessment

Re-score the risk AFTER considering the effectiveness of existing controls to determine the residual risk level.

Financial Capital

23a-1
Residual Total Revenue Impact Dropdown
Score the residual impact on Total Revenue on a 5-point dropdown (Very Low, Low, Medium, High, Very High), after factoring in the effectiveness of existing controls and treatment actions. The same percentage bands used for inherent assessment (e.g., ≤ 1.5%, > 1.5%–3%, > 3%–5%, > 5%–7.5%, > 7.5%) will be available as reference text via a tooltip on the Residual Financial Capital Impact field.
23a-2
Residual EBITDA Impact Dropdown
Score the residual impact on EBITDA using the same 5-point dropdown (Very Low, Low, Medium, High, Very High). The underlying EBITDA ranges (≤ 4%, > 4%–8%, > 8%–12%, > 12%–16%, > 16%) are also provided as reference in the tooltip attached to the Residual Financial Capital Impact field.
23a-3
Residual Leverage (Net Debt / EBITDA) Impact Dropdown
Score the residual impact on Leverage (Net debt to EBITDA, medium term) using the 5-point dropdown (Very Low, Low, Medium, High, Very High). The same reference thresholds (≤ 1x, > 1–2x, > 2–3x, > 3–4x, > 4x) will be available as a tooltip on the Residual Financial Capital Impact field.
23a
Residual Financial Capital Impact Dropdown
Overall residual impact on Financial Capital on a 5-point scale (Very Low, Low, Medium, High, Very High). A tooltip on this field consolidates all reference descriptions from the Financial Capital Risk Impact Scale (Total Revenue, EBITDA and Leverage parameter bands) to guide the assessor while selecting the residual impact level.
23a-4
Residual Risk Appetite / Tolerance Dropdown
Auto-populated classification based on the residual Financial Capital position and thresholds: Within risk appetite, Risk appetite breached, but within risk tolerance, or Risk tolerance breached. This value is derived together with the residual impact assessment after considering the values recorded against each of the capital impact fields (23a–23e).

Multi-Capital Residual Assessment

For all capitals (Financial, Compliance, Human, Natural, Intellectual), residual impact will be scored through parameter-based 5-point dropdowns (Very Low to Very High), with the underlying criteria captured as reference tooltips. Capital-specific residual risk appetite and tolerance thresholds will be configured so that appetite / threshold calculations can be applied consistently to each capital and rolled up into the overall residual impact and risk assessment.

23b
Residual Compliance Capital Impact Dropdown
Residual impact on regulatory & legal compliance — Very Low (1) to Very High (5), using parameter-based scoring and Compliance-specific appetite / tolerance thresholds.
23c
Residual Human Capital Impact Dropdown
Residual impact on people, safety, and workforce — Very Low (1) to Very High (5), supported by Human Capital-specific parameters and appetite / tolerance thresholds.
23d
Residual Natural Capital Impact Dropdown
Residual impact on environmental and natural resources — Very Low (1) to Very High (5), with Natural Capital parameters and associated appetite / tolerance thresholds.
23e
Residual Intellectual Capital Impact Dropdown
Residual impact on intellectual property, R&D, and knowledge assets — Very Low (1) to Very High (5), using parameter-based scoring and Intellectual Capital appetite / tolerance thresholds.
23
Residual Impact (Overall) Dropdown
Overall residual impact score (1–5) selected by the assessor, typically aligned to the highest residual impact across capitals (23a–23e) and informed by capital-level appetite / tolerance thresholds.
24
Residual Likelihood (1–5) Dropdown
Likelihood of occurrence after considering controls — Very Low (1) to Very High (5), using the same probability bands as inherent but reassessed in light of treatment effectiveness.
25
Residual Risk Score Auto-Calculated
= (Residual Impact × Residual Likelihood) + Velocity — auto-calculated using the selected Residual Impact (23), Residual Likelihood (24) and the Velocity score from Phase 2.
26
Residual Risk Rating Select
High ≥ 18 Medium 8–17 Low/Acceptable < 8 — mapped from the Residual Risk Score to indicate the final residual risk level.
27
Reportable As Dropdown
Functional Level risk or Entity Level risk
28
Reportable To Text
Free-text field to specify the reporting audience (e.g., EC & RMC, Functional Review & DOR, Board)

👁️ Phase 5 — Monitor & Re-assessment

Risks will be continuously monitored and re-assessed as needed to ensure controls remain effective and the risk profile stays current.

7
Monitor & Re-assessment
Risks will be monitored on an ongoing basis. Residual risk scores will be re-assessed as needed — triggered by changes in control effectiveness, business conditions, or risk environment. Escalation follows the governance structure.

📦 Phase 6 — Archival Approval

Before a risk can be archived, it must go through a formal approval process with documented justification.

8a
Archival Approval
Before archiving, the Risk Owner / Risk Champion must review and approve the archival request. This ensures no risk is archived without proper governance sign-off.
8b
Archival Rationale Paragraph
Mandatory justification for why the risk is being archived. Document the rationale — e.g., risk fully mitigated, risk accepted and within appetite, activity terminated, or risk no longer relevant due to changed business conditions.
9
Archive
Once approved, the risk is archived. Retained for audit trail. Archived risks can be reactivated if conditions change.
